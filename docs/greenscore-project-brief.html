The Problem

Why Americans can't fix what they can't see

🚗

Car Dependency

America is built around cars. Transportation is the #1 source of US carbon emissions, yet most people have zero visibility into their personal contribution.

🛒

Overconsumption

5% of the world's population produces 30% of its waste. Same-day delivery and fast fashion have made buying and discarding the default behavior.

🍔

Food Waste

The US wastes 80 million tons of food per year. Most ends up in landfills producing methane — yet grocery decisions feel invisible and consequence-free.

🧠

The Awareness Gap

People know climate change is real but don't connect daily habits to global impact. There's no immediate feedback loop between action and consequence.

The Solution
GreenScore is like MyFitnessPal for your carbon footprint — log your day, see your impact in trees, beat the US average, and get AI-powered swaps that actually save you money.

Features

What you're building

01

🌡️ Daily Carbon Dashboard

A clean homepage with a speedometer going green to red. Shows today's CO₂ score and live comparison to the US daily average of 47kg.

Build First
02

📝 Activity Logger

3 simple daily inputs: Transport (drove/bus/walked), Food (meat/veggie/vegan), Shopping (online order/groceries/clothes). Like logging meals in MyFitnessPal.

Build First
03

🌳 Trees Visualization

Convert all emissions into trees needed to absorb your impact. Show your forest vs the average American's forest. Dense vs sparse. Emotional and instantly understood.

Build First
04

🤖 AI Tip Engine

After each log entry, Claude gives one personalized swap suggestion with exact CO₂ savings. "Your burger = 3.5kg. Swap to chicken, save 2kg." Powered by Anthropic API.

Build First
05

📸 Receipt Scanner

User snaps a photo of their grocery receipt. AI reads items and auto-calculates emissions per product. No manual logging. This is your demo centerpiece moment.

Wow Factor
06

🏙️ Local Impact View

"At your current rate, your city's beach floods by 2075." Tie emissions to their actual location. Local = emotional. Global = ignored.

Wow Factor
07

📈 Weekly Progress Chart

7-day bar chart showing green vs red days. One clear insight: "You were 20% greener than last week." Simple, readable, motivating.

Build Second
08

⚖️ Nature's Debt Invoice

Frame emissions as a debt: "You owe 438 trees today. You paid back 12. Still owe 426." Debt feels urgent in a way percentages never do.

Build Second

"At this rate your child will inherit summers 4°F hotter." Makes it personal, not abstract.

A polar bear that looks happy on green days and sad on bad ones. People get attached. Attachment drives retention.

"847 people in your city drove alone today, dropping your neighborhood's air quality." Local = emotional.

"You're in the top 15% of your city." Nobody wants to be the worst on their street. Shame and pride both drive behavior.

🌳 The Tree Math

1 tree absorbs ~21kg CO₂/year = 0.057kg/day. So: Trees needed = Your Emissions ÷ 0.057. A burger (3.5kg) needs 61 trees. Average American day (47kg) needs 824 trees.

Build Order

How to spend your hackathon hours

Hour 1–2

Setup + API Connections

React project, Carbon Interface API key, Open Food Facts, Anthropic API. Get data flowing before building UI.

Hour 3–5

Dashboard + Activity Logger

The core loop. Log activity → see CO₂ → see trees. This is the MVP. Get this working first.

Hour 6–8

Trees Visualization + AI Tips

Your two wow factors. Animate the forest. Wire up the Anthropic API for instant swap suggestions.

Hour 9–10

Weekly Chart + Carbon Budget

Progress bar, 7-day chart, nature's debt invoice. Makes the app feel complete and data-rich.

Hour 11–12

Receipt Scanner

The demo centerpiece. Upload grocery photo → AI reads items → shows CO₂ per product. Judges will love this moment.

Hour 13–14

Polish + Bug Fixes

Clean up the UI, fix edge cases, make the demo flow smooth. Don't add features here.

Hour 15–16

Demo Preparation

Practice your 2-minute pitch. Dashboard → log burger → AI tip → receipt scan → weekly chart → impact statement.
